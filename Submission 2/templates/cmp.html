Optical Flow - Motion Estimation


Optical flow is a specific method within motion estimation that focuses on calculating the velocity or motion vectors of pixels
in an image or between consecutive frames. It helps determine the direction and speed of object motion in an image sequence.

Motion estimation is a computer vision technique used to analyze and quantify the movement of objects in a sequence of images or frames.
It involves tracking the displacement of pixels or features between consecutive frames to understand how objects or scenes change over time.
